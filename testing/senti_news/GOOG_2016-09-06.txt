Date: 2016-09-06
A chief executive’s political leanings may significantly influence whom workers donate to and choose at the polls, a study found.
Andrew Ross Sorkin DealBook column reports on academic study finding power of chief executives over politics of their employees is stunning and potentially disturbing; says political preferences of CEOs are among biggest factors influencing both campaign contributions and electoral choices of workers; presidential election of 2016, however, appears to be topic most executives are shying away from, most likely due to nasty volatility of discourse that has emerged over course of campaign cycle.
